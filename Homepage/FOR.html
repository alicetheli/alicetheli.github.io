Frame of Reference (FOR)

Frame Of Reference, FOR, is very important! Because frame of reference helps the occupational therapist to stay organize with their specialty!
Frame of Reference contains these FIVE things:

  1. Population

    Who are your clients? Do the clients have mental, physical or a social need?
  2. Continuum of function/dysfunction:

    Where is the client level in the spectrum of function or dysfunction?
  3. Theory Regarding Change:
  4. Role of the practitioner:
  5. Assessment Instructions: What kind of evaluation should the practitioner do? Interview, observation or standard test? For example in the geriatric OTs look at how well a patient can maintain their balance and how well do they do their ADLs.
