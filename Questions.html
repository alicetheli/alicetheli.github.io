Interview questions

I organized the question based on how frequently the question was asked and which school asked which question. I also provide my answers to each of the questions. I hope by doing this, you can still ace the interview even if you have limited time to practice your interview.

Quick Strategies

  • Remember the words that you used in your resume because those words are probably the strongest words that you can think of to make you shine.
  • Incorporate you applications into your interview!

I promised you that by doing this two strategies, you can outline the content of your interview faster.

Content

MUST DOs
Questions by schools

If you are super busy, at least do the questions from the MUST DOs and Questions by Schools because these questions can potentially come up in your interview

MUST DOs

MUST DO because interviewers from different schools ask these questions.

  1. Why do you want to become an OT?
  2. Why do you apply to this program?
  3. What achievement are you the most proud of?
  4. What is your weakness?(OR. What would you like to change about yourself?)
  5. How would you handle the stress of OT school?

Questions by schools

Here are the questions asked by the admission staff of Huntington, Rush, Salus and Thomas Jefferson University.

  1. What is the most important quality that an OT must have?
  2. How will an OTD degree help you in the future?
  3. What do you think about the mission trips?(Any Contact with the poor)
  4. Choose one adjective that describes you and tell me about yourself
  5. Tell me about your observation experience
  6. Tell me a time when your observed patients does not have faith
  7. Name a location such as, east or west coast, as your ideal OT program location.
  8. Ten years after you have your license, what would you like to do?
  9. Differentiate a PT from an OT
  10. Do you ever consider other professions besides OT?
  11. What is your college major?
  12. Describe your method of learning. How does this fit with the Occupational/Physical Therapy program
  13. Why do you want to come to XYZ city?
  14. Explain why you have your bad grades.
  15. Tell me an instance in which you asked for help.
  16. What will OT look like in ten years?
  17. Group Activity: Make an OT program for participants such as, paroles, children who have autism,and the elderlies, to raise their health awareness and increase socialization
    • What would you do if your participants refuse to participate?
    • What would you do if your participant has a disability that prevents them from engaging into the program?
    • How is your program related to OT?
  18. “Collaborate with your group members and write a three sentence response for this question in ten minutes:
    If you walked into a hospital room to work with a patient and he wanted to wait until a TV program was over, how would you handle the situation?”
  19. Tell the interviewer how this group activity is different from the group project that you do in school
